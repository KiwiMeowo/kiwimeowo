Fixiations

Minecraft

Minecraft was one of the biggest things in my childhood, it introduced a lot of internet culture, friends, and even my motivation to start my art journey! Even when I'm not as passionate about it as when I'm younger, I could never forget about the things that changed me forever.

Minecraft PE

So back when Minecraft was booming, I was told the existence of the Minecraft. I played Minecraft PE, which does not have the quality like the desktop version of course. However, I quickly became fascinated with the freedom to build basically everything. Houses, farms, conveyor belts (water stream), rollercoasters...

While I spent a lot of time without Minecraft Java, I still had a lot of fun playing it. Because a lot of blocks are missing in PE, I had to make compromises by using other similar blocks or just forget about them, I was quite sad item frames weren't a thing back then. But somehow, I still loved the stuff I built. I even built a whole city, with ice rinks, hotels, schools and stuff. As a little kid, I find it soothing to roleplay as a citizen living inside there, even if it is going to that school.

Another thing about Minecraft PE is that there was no day night cycle in creative mode back then. While the buildings looked good, I found it a bit lonely to see the same things with the same colors. But a maracle happened, not sure which update was that, but one time I was playing the world, and I got to see the sun set for the very first time. It was one of the most beautiful things I've seen in Minecraft.

Minecraft: Java Edition

On a birthday, I got a Minecraft Java account. I got to play with new blocks and play at famous servers, such as Mineplex, Hypixel, and HiveMC. With so much freedom given to me, I got to build really cool stuff in my own world, like building a cave house (I really like cave houses, I guess I like small houses), some random houses, torture devices food making machines.

But the best part of playing Minecraft Java Edition is the servers, where I found many online friends. Being a kid with repressed anger issues, I annoyed a lot of private rooms in Mineplex. Somehow, that got me some very kind friends. And after joining the Mineplex forum, I learnt about the ways of making online friends. I even started drawing because a person named MishyMolly made a post about how to draw a Minecraft character, and encouraged me when I tried to draw my skin.

I spent an incredible amount of time playing the servers, playing the minigames and horribly failing any pvp games. There was one time I nearly won a bridges game, but lost because I got stuck in the air due to lag. But no matter the outcome, I always enjoyed waiting for my friends to come online and playing with them.

Minecraft is the reason I started drawing, Kyrea existed, I learnt about a lot of Internet stuff and even made friends, both online and real-life. Because of my Minecraft fixiation, I got to bond with some of my primary school friends, finally finding some common interests with them. While most of the primary school experience was bitter, at least the last few years were enjoyable.
