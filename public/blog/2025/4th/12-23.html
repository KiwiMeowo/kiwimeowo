Dried up pens

Consequences of not using the pens I bought

Tags: Discussion, Life
235 words (1 min read)

During my childhood, I have a fondness over stationeries, I love collecting stuff that might help with drawing, or just stuff that is both cute and useful. I have plenty of colored pens in my collection, but I rarely use them as I draw with my pencil more. As a result, the pens becomes clogged up, and is basically not useable.

The solutions I found online are often:

  • Put pen in hot water/alcohol
  • Heat up the pen
  • Blow air into the tube

However, none of those worked, or the results lasted a very short amount of time. Looking at the cool pens I have, especially the ones with multi-colored ink, I feel very guilty about wasting them.

So today, I saw a Instagram reel, a new solution where you stuff the tube with moldable eraser, pushing the ink out using the pressure. I was so excited to test this method, and guess what? It worked very well, the ink flowed better than when I first bought them (That might mean they get used up faster... But oh well I have to use them up at some point anyways). Even for the white pens (which ink is always very faint) they worked very well after I stuff the erasers into the tube.

So that concludes my very short blog post, I love any viewers here who owns lots of dried up pens can try this method.
