Bliss of Listening

The woes of being in an quiet office

Weather: Hot, but cold indoors

Listening to: Nothing

Mood: Sleepy

Random thought: I wonder how my interning classmates are doing

Tags: Life, Vent429 words (2 min read)

My internship has recently started, I find it quite lucky that I got to find an internship quicker than most classmates. While I was nervous at first, I was still excited to learn what work actually is outside of being told that “boss will yell at me”.

Now, how do I think? It actually isn’t as horrible as I thought, the collegues are cool, my intern peers seems to be nice, and so far I have not gotten in trouble yet. However, if I have to mention one thing I dislike, is that it is just so quiet here.

Because everyone is focusing on work, the most I would hear is the printer noises, and white noise, and maybe the occasional discussions about a project. Because the office is a professional setting, it is best to be serious and do what you gotta do, so no earphones and music.

It makes sense, it doesn’t sound formal to plug in earphones while the phone from other colleagues might call you. However, I realised how much I enjoy listening to song while I do my own thing. When I am drawing, writing, or just chilling on the bed, very likely I am also listening to a song or a youtuber rambling about a game I’ve never heard of. The work I am given so far is rather simple, but whenever I input data into spreadsheets, I really wished I can just listen to my playlist at the same time.

I think what’s worse is when I am out of things to do. Sometimes I finish the work I’m given, then my mentor would say there is nothing for me at the moment. So I would have to sit on my chair all day doing nothing, or maybe do reports (which there is not a lot to write about now). While I can scroll my phone and play games, I feel very bad like, what if someone very high up comes and see me, who has done everything, “slacking off”? So the best thing I could do is pretend I am working (staring at the spreadsheet documents), or just space out.

So after work, I learnt to enjoy listening to music more than I already have. Whenever I am free, I would quickly plug in my earphones and listen to the songs I want to listen to for so long. It feels like the songs sound extra beautiful recently, it’s sad there’s less than 8 hours of free time now.

I hope the internship will go well, and I don’t go insane.
