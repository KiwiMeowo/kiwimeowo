I don't know how I became less shy

Huh...

Weather: Cool... Bro why is it so cool?

Listening to: TV show noises

Mood: Tired

Random thought: Exam finally finished sighhhhh

Tags: Life

When I was a kid, I was the shy type, I speak quietly, I don't like talking to people. It's standard introverted kid thing maybe, or maybe it's because I was bullied idk. So growing up, I tend to have a lot of trouble in presentations and talking to strangers. Everytime I have to speak in front of a crowd, I often speak too quietly and my brain couldn't focus from the stress.

Aside from the counselling I have, I never really learnt to cope with the stress of being in social environments. However, I had something which I never understood how, which is getting over the shyness I have. Ever since going to university, I don't appear as timid as before, and when I have to do presentations, I seem to able to manage it quite well.

Being told that I am introverted my entire life, I often picture myself as a shy person. I find my fear of talking to people quite annoying, it feels like it is the thing stopping me from being a functional person. And for some reason, when I confessed my situation, people often say I don't act as socially anxious as most socially anxious people. But how though, was the counselling that effective?

Honestly, I'm quite glad I've gotten this ability to act more confident. Because of this, I have less trouble dealing with events involve with dealing with people, and because of that, I don't have to deal with the shit of people complaining about me being too shy. This trait I have isn't perfect though, when I join events like gathering with university students, I might act chill on the outside, but I don't talk as much as an extroverted person would, so people around me will still point out I don't speak a lot.

Another unfortunate thing is that this trait consumes a lot of my energy. After I finish a presentation or interview, I often end up feeling extremely burnt out. This is what I am feeling right now, I had two interviews in a week, now I don't feel very right, ugh I'm so tired. I'm blessed to randomly have this ability, but man it sure is exhausting.
