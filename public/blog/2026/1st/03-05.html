Around last week, I was having a hard time sleeping due to my air-con making too much noise. In the next morning, I woke up with a somewhat fuzzy head, and suddenly gained a thought: I should get earrings!

|TW| Mentions of needles

For most of my life, having ear piercings is a neutral thing to me. I wouldn't say I don't like them, but I'm not the type to dress up myself, and it felt too bothersome to wear them during secondary school (since they're not allowed). After I attended university, where the dress rules are far less strict, it seems logical to try something new with my body, but my brain seemed to not realise that, not that I have the motivation to do it anyways.

Another thing about me is that I like tiny stuff, and shiny stuff. If it weren't for the fact gems are expensive, I would hoard them (There are toy gems too but not all of them are shiny). I guess apparently that night my brain clicked, "Yo what if... gems on ears?", so that's why I compulsively bought earrings online the moment I woke up.

Blood donation trouble

As you may know, in fear of blood-transmitted diseases, people who had done piercings in the last 3 months (longer or shorter depending on which blood donation centre you go to) cannot donate blood. As it has been enough time after my last blood donation, I decided to go there before I do the piercing.

Unfortunately, that day before, I got bit by my hamster, quite roughly I'd say. The blood donation centre advised me to let my wound heal before going there, and that might take around a week. A bit annoyed, but it's kinda my fault I didn't pay attention to my hamster's mood properly. So I booked the blood donation one week later, and the ear piercing the day after that.

Fast forward to the day before blood donation, I just got my packages, including the earrings, some other items, and needle-felt wool. I was kinda bored at the time, so I played around with felting, and you can guess what happened. After being stabbed by the needle, of course I can't go to donate my blood, it felt like my carefully planned schedule fell apart. Blood donation is somewhat a thing I look forward to, something to do during free time, a good thing to do for society (even if I hate humans), and free snacks, so I was quite bummed. I spent the day walking around the library and park instead, saw some pretty story books.

On the brighter side, the fact I just happened to get injured the day before blood donation has to be some kind of divine intervention. I didn't get it at the time, but after I did the piercings, it kinda makes sense now.

Piercing experience

There are two forms of piercings, piercing guns or needles. Piercings guns are cheaper, while needles heal faster, more accurate and hurt less. To describe their prices, using piercing guns are priced around HKD$200-300, while needles might cost $900+. Needles are terribly expensive, but as a first-timer, and not wanting to get constant imflammation, I would want to try the needles first.

So after I got there, I signed the forms, picked the earrings I like the most (I picked a shiny butterfly one with embedded pink gems, pretty,,,), and sat down on the chair with the adjustable backs. They marked the spot on my earlobes, numbed my ears with ice, then used the needle to puncture a hole.

To describe the pain, on the scale of 1 (Almost forgettable pain) to 10 (AAAAAA), I would say 4, one point higher than regular blood drawing prosedure, and the same pain level when the nurse drawing my blood put the needle more roughly. And because the pain was mostly instantaneous, it wasn't as scary and painful as I thought. They quickly completed piercing both sides, and I've gotten pretty butterfly gems on my ears!

When they started treating my piercings, this conversation happened that I find quite funny:

Piercer: The procedure is done, tell me in case you are feeling dizzy!

Me: Ah, that's something that can happen?

Me: Good thing you told me, because that's exactly I'm feeling right now

It's like comedic timing

The piercers reacted quickly to help me feel better, they moved down the back of the chair and let me lie down, they also gave me a candy. Within a few minutes, I already felt better. Apparently, this is called vasovagal syncope, an reaction to stress and pain, and it can make you sick, dizzy, hot, and even pass out. This is something that can happen when piercing, but it can also trigger if you stand for too long and blood drawing, well that explains the times I feel dizzy standing on the train.

They gave me a some advice on how to take care of my piercings, and some cleaning solutions to clean them. I went home quite satisfied, though the vasovagal syncope did drain my energy a lot.

So far, there's not much complications, just a little red around the wound and a bit of bleeding, which are all normal.I have to wait for at least 2 months before I can wear other earrings, so for now I'm stuck with my current ones. While I love the butterfly earrings I chose, I somtimes wish I could choose to get their entire collection, man shiny rocks are pretty.

For now, here's my plushies with my earrings.
