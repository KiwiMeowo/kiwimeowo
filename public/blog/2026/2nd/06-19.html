TADC: The Last Act

While the post will be released on 6 June 2026, this blog is written on 11 June 2026, because I want to write down the cinema experience on the day I watched the movie (4 June 2026).

When EP9 finally started, I was glad to see the intro from the pilot appeared again. When the circus members was playing the intro, it was before Pomni arrived and Kaufmo just abstracted. And this time, we finally got to see Pomni play the intro! But it was at the cost of Caine being deleted, and everyone being overwhelmed...

Day after- Day after- Day after- Day after- Day after- Day after- Day after- Day after- Day after- Day after-

After Kinger admitted that he accidentally deleted Caine, Jax was pissed that Kinger did the worst possible thing and let them to possibly suffer in the empty circus for eternity. And in that tense situation, Kinger revealed an information that may be harder to handle: He found a folder containing the neuro data of the circus members, which he realised Scratch, one of the programmers, was making a way to import minds into a digital world to continue living if he died from cancer. And that means every single circus members aren't exactly got trapped into this world, but they are just copies of their human counterpart's mind. With this major information revealed, everyone had an awful existental crises and wandered off into different ways to process all that happened.

The Digital Copies theory, or whatever it's called, is a fairly common theory among the TADC community. This plotpoint has existed in other stories (I don't know the name, but I vaguely remember a playthrough of a game with a similar premise), and it makes a lot of sense this theory would take place. When the twist is revealed, I wasn't too surprised, but I was quite happy it happened, it is a good way to execute the main theme of finding hope in hopelessness.

Pomni, seeing everyone breaking down and disturbed, decided to go look for Kinger first, whom she found the most reliable. With the ringmaster gone, Kinger started repairing the circus using his imagination powers. Pomni tried to help Kinger a bit, and eventually broke down in tears under the current stress. Kinger, being the usual mentor, supported Pomni and told her how adaptable she is at the circus, only taking a few days to get used to everything. Kinger then said "You are very strong, Pomni, and I know you can get through this", the exact phrase he said in EP3. Kinger you absolute GOAT.

Til this day, I'm still flabbergasted that Pomni actually only took a few days to get used to the circus. I always assumed there's a bigger time gap between the episodes 1 to 3, it's very hard to believe anyone can handle being trapped there so easily. Man Pomni's emotional quotient is very high.

As Kinger and Pomni started cleaning up, slowly other circus members reached out as well. With the efforts of the circus members, the place slowly gets repaired... Except for Jax, who initally talked to Pomni then left again. Sulking alone like usual, huh, I said. Then Pomni said "I wish I had asked what he wanted to say...", oh shit he's gonna die. Next scene, Jax is abstracted. No crash out scene, no emotional breakdowns, no dramatic reveals. Last moment Jax was walking away, then now he's an abstracted entity.

The fact there isn't a grand reveal of Jax turning into an abstraction is certainly an interesting choice. Jax, being a popular character, would bring a lot of angst and sadness if the movie explictly showed him painfully (or peacefully, if you think abstraction is like the one in EP7) abstracting, but the movie didn't. Perhaps Gooseworx didn't want to put emphasis on suffering in this episode?

Seeing his abstracted form, the circus members are all baffled, Zooble especially said "Of course he has to abstract during this time!". Gangle was upset in a way, but she felt bad that she couldn't cry for Jax's abstraction. And Ragatha seems to be the most visibly upset, as she felt responsible for Jax's situation. Apparently back then, she wanted to talk with Jax regarding Ribbit after Ribbit's abstraction, but he refused to even talk about it, and made Ragatha promise to not bring up Ribbit ever again.

To comfort Ragatha, Pomni told Ragatha her appreciation after all this time. Ragatha is the friend that always supported Pomni ever since the beginning. And with a Thank You, they gave each other a heart-warming hug (like 30 seconds long?), and went separate ways, Ragatha going to keep others safe, and Pomni dealing with Jax.

As someone who enjoys Ragatha and Pomni's dynamic, I feel like the hug scene felt a bit... underwhelming. It isn't really a movie problem, but it stems from the fact there isn't enough screentime to watch them bond more. In most times we see them together, it's usually showing how they couldn't get along at first, like Ragatha trying too hard being positive, or Ragatha worrying if Pomni hates her. And in the moments where they can reconcile, it's like a moment long. Maybe if TADC had been longer, we could've seen more character dynamics...

Next scene, we see Jax (in his abstracted form) in the dormroom hallway, and Pomni, with a gun. However, the gun wasn't targeted at Jax, but at the lights above. Pomni seemed to have a theory abstractions get calmer under darkness after hearing Kinger's backstory, and she put it into practice... And it worked, the moment there's no more light, Jax stopped charging towards Pomni, and simply stood in front of her. In this moment, Pomni hugged him.

Apparently some people laughed when they saw the gun, I mean it's always absurd to see cartoon characters with a gun. For me, I thought this scene would reference something from EP6, but I guess there isn't.

Pomni found herself in the mind of Jax, she sees several doors. And in each door, there is a false impression of the circus members, and a false version of himself. One door shows that after Ragatha abstracted, Pomni tried to comfort Jax, saying she could fix him (good lord), but Jax strangled her with a terrifying expression, and she eventually ran away in fear.

From this scene, I feel like this door shows that Jax thinks Pomni just wants to make Jax change, like redeeming a villain. But since Jax didn't want anyone peering into his mind, he made a persona to protect his inner self. And for this version, it makes him look like a character with pure evil, no redeeming qualities at all, so that nobody would try to fix him at all.

The second door shows Gangle wearing her manager mask, ordering everyone to prepare for Zooble funeral. Then Jax appears, proudly in an maid outfit. Apparently he claimed he was wearing this ironically, calling this genderfreaky (yes this is actually said in the movie), and he flirted with Gangle, for some reason.

There's quite a lot to unpack here, more than I initally thought. The first thing I thought when I saw this scene is that he is trying to fix the time he was forced into a maid outfit. Back then he reacted a lot and was very flustered about it, so in this scene, he acts he likes it sacastically, so that nobody can poke fun at his insecurity anymore.

After reading some analysis, there are several other things I've missed. Aside from the maid outfit, Gangle was also wearing her manager mask, which is the episode where Jax couldn't get to control her at all, so there are two things that represent the times Jax couldn't be in control. However, this scene takes place in the imaginary situation of Zooble's abstraction, who is the main protector and friend of Gangle. And with them gone, Jax is completely free to abuse her in any way. Using this imagination, he could regain his control from these two past events, allowing him to abuse Gangle more despite her wearing the manager mask and him wearing the maid outfit. Combining all that, I think this scene represents his toxic self esculating his abuse towards Gangle, even sexual harassing her, despite the two factors that used to stop him, because Zooble is gone.

The third one is in a Looney Tunes style, Zooble was mad at Jax because Gangle abstracted, so they chase after Jax, only to fall off a cliff like a coyote.

I think this represents Jax not taking Zooble seriously at all, because they look too cartoonish.

The next room Pomni enters contains the three Jaxes she met, plus one that is trapped playing the piano. She wanted to go to the room with the real Jax, but none of them wanted to cooperate. However, the Jaxes start disappearing one by one, by the time the piano Jax remained, he handed the key for the final room to Pomni, then disappeared.

I'm not very certain what the piano Jax represents, maybe if Gooseworx is willing, she might explain the plotpoints more.

In the final room, it's basically Jax's story starting from right before the cirus. After running away from home, Jax suddenly found himself in the circus. Like normal people, he started panicking. Luckily, Ribbit and Kaufmo came to help him adapt to this life. Slowly, Jax warmed up to the group, and even became closer to Ribbit.

Something about Ribbit and Kaufmo is that they feel very ordinary, like you can imagine this type of people in real life. I remember Kaufmo saying something along the lines of "She is a bad role model, she will corrupt you!", and it really brought me back to the old days of secondary school, where I hung out with a group of friends who already knew each other well. I do feel a bit bad for Kaufmo, it's like the situation of your friend being closer to their new friend, and you are more alone now.

During Jax and Ribbit's sleepover, Ribbit opened up and revealed her past to Jax: She came from a Mormon family, but eventually ran away from home, yet she often wishes she could see her parents again. And for Jax, he let his guard down as well and revealed his life as well: After his parents divorced, he stayed with his father but his relationship with him was distant. He decided to stay with his mother, but because of the divorce, his mother became abusive, often verbally insulting him. One day, he said something he shouldn't, his mother was laughing at first, then it turned into anger, then suddenly, she hugged him. Unused to the sudden affection, he pushed her away, then she became unconscious from the fall. Fearing that he had accidentally killed her, he ran away.

After sharing the story, Ribbit comforted Jax, said if he killed his mom, the police would've been after him (Hey maybe it's just the police couldn't find him lol). This scene shows the bond between the two up to this point, she even place her bowtie onto his head, seeing Jax being flustered is really wholesome.

After seeing Jax being an insufferable prick for eight episodes straight, it's quite strange to see Jax acting more open. And the ribbon scene is really sweet, and perhaps the most unsubtle hint about Jax's identity. While being trans and simply being insecure about his feminity are two different possible scenarios, the reason I'm leaning more onto him being trans because Gooseworx is also trans, and I think there's a high chance she would want to represent this. (Despite this implication, I'll still refer to him as he/him as this isn't 100% confirmed, and I don't know Jax would want to reveal this part of him yet). And for his backstory, it's clear the reason Jax avoids affection and being vulnerable to anyone stems from his family, family can impact on your personality a lot. Especially he was abused by his mother a lot, being vulnerable to a high risk of being hurt again.

*28 Jun edit: Gooseworx has confirmed that Jax is a closeted transwomen, I have been seeing a lot of fanart of Jax in feminine outfit, which is very wholesome

While they are still in the room, Kaufmo knocked on the door, and Jax IMMEDIATELY threw the ribbon away, in fear of him seeing him like that. And after that day, Jax avoided Ribbit, and joined Kaufmo instead during team games. And when Ribbit came to confront him, he kept avoiding talking about that night, pretending nothing he said was true. And after avoiding her so much, she became depressed, and eventually abstracted. Kaufmo and Ragatha tried to comfort him, but he ended up pushing them away and refused to talk about it. Later on, when Kaufmo told him about a potential exit, Jax refused to go with him, and later on Kaufmo abstracted from the panic.

It's pretty tragic that the fact Jax refused to open up with others, it not only hurt him, but those who wanted to help as well. Because of his actions, the people who cared about him either felt really hurt or abstracted. And this didn't make Jax feel better, him bottling up only makes him suffer more and more. And eventually he abstracted, it's a cautionary tale on why you shouldn't reject the vulnerable side of yourself.

Pomni finally met the true Jax, who was standing near a pink lantern. Like usual, he kept telling Pomni to leave him, but this time he's visibly more distressed. Not listening to his words, Pomni ran to Jax, and finally giving him the hug he needed for this whole time. Unlike the last time, Jax did not struggle, and started crying. "I don't want to go" he said, and his body started glowing after his abstracted form accidentally triggered a flashbang.

Pomni is really admirable, she's willing to listen to anyone in the circus if given the chance. And when Jax tried to put on his mask, trying to be emotionally distant, she kept trying to approach him, no matter what. And even when Jax has abstracted, she still tried to reach out, which somewhat called back to EP2. This moment also reminded me what Kinger said: "The worst thing you could do, is to make people think they're unloved or unwanted", and Pomni tried many times to make sure Jax doesn't feel unwanted.

"Jax, I like your writing but I can't wait for you to die"

- KiwiMeowo, 11 June 2026

If you don't know, I have a love-hate relationship with Jax's character, the love is more about my curiousity, and my hate is a bit stronger. Because I was a poor bullied kid when I was young, I always despised bullies, real and fictional. In terms of fiction, there are there types of bullies I see:

  1. The not-to-be-taken-seriously: Torments others, but it's ok because it's funny
  2. The redeemed: Used to be a bully, but gets redeemed and forgiven
  3. The punching bag: Usually the extreme evil type, sometimes over the top. Then they get killed brutally.

To be fair, I don't often finish the character arcs of these types of characters because I'd get too much bad feelings before I can even finish the story, so there may be false judgements. But as a victim, none of these characters bring me much positive feelings. For the first one, while the jokes can be funny sometimes, if you look at it one percent more seriously, it becomes a bit upsetting. For the third one, while it's usually a way for victims to vent out their frustration, it doesn't make me feel better because I know it's a cope, I know the bullies are living fine while I have to suffer alone. For the second one, while it can be more realistic and offers a second perspective on how bullies may also be struggling, I just couldn't comprehend forgiving them. They've done too much damage, couldn't you just let me not forgive them?

After seeing Jax through out the episodes, I imagined a new type of bully character that may satisfy me. A bully who is as awful as Jax, yet has a story that brings sympathy, and in the end, the character dies, not in a grand sacrifice or a just punishment, just a mundane one, like dying to their dumb mistakes. It's a cruel story, there isn't a good moral story if the bully, who didn't get the chance to better themselves, just dies this way. But I wondered, what would I feel when I see a character like Jax to die like this?

Jax abstracting is fully under my expectations, and I did mention I want to see him abstract, but I feel somewhat conflicted. On one hand, I do feel like it's his fault that he ended up like this, he chose to push away others, and he hurt so many people, the fact he abstracted is entirely his fault. On the other, there is this melancholic feeling. The moment he said he didn't want to go, something stirred in me. He spent a lot of his time tormenting himself and others, and when he finally showed his true side, it's already too late for him. I still think he deserved it, it's still sad to see this is how he goes.

Maybe, it might be better the fact I feel conflicted seeing him abstract. Nothing is black and white in life, and one of the things I hate seeing in fiction is painting the bad guys too one-dimensional or forcing us to forgive them. I don't know if you agree, but the story didn't make us entirely forgive him, it only gives us a new perspective.

Suddenly, Pomni was grabbed by a chain of hands, and got rescued by the other circus members, they decided to help Pomni and Jax together. Using the knowledge of abtractions being more stable in the dark, they built a tent with a dark interior, and brought Jax inside. Seeing Jax entering the tent, Gangle felt overwhelmed and started crying, and Zooble hugged her said something along the lines of "It's ok, it's what makes us human".

I'm not sure what exactly Gangle was feeling at that time. Did she feel joy for Jax safely inside the tent, or frustration that he didn't get a bigger punishment, or sadness he became this way? I don't really know how to analyse how she's feeling, because I feel kinda complicated as well.

While the circus members are resting, Caine was peeking from the corner... CAINE???? It turns out that deleting him only sent him to the void. While he was stuck at first, but slowly, he found out the way he can slowly get his way from the void to the Internet. And while he was breaking the jail, it is also shown it's exactly how he managed to break containment and create the circus when he was young.

This scene reminded me one of the complaints people had, which is in EP8, the words "I can solve anything, I can solve this one! This is what it is, a puzzle I need to solve!" felt a bit too strange, why puzzle? After seeing that scene, him bringing up puzzles makes sense, since him breaking out from the jail/void feels a lot like solving a puzzle!

After he reached the Internet, he grabbed a list of names of the circus members, and typed one onto the search engine. He scrolled the person's social media, and learnt about her life. He felt an immense guilt, they had a life outside, but all he did was torment them. Then his corrupted side showed up, saying the circus members should've listened to Caine. After some contemplating, Caine decided to let Abel go, he's finally free from Caine. After Abel floated away, Caine flew back to the circus as he met the Moon. The Moon comforts him, and Caine asks how did she learn to say "I love you" when he never told her the phrase.

This is one of my complaints regarding the movie: How did Caine survive being deleted? While I don't know how commands work, especially within the circus, it feels a bit too convenient that Caine only ended up in the void as opposed to being wiped out entirely. The scene where Caine gets accidentally deleted is really impactful, and the fact Caine managed to come back so easily kinda brings down the impact. It's somewhat like reviving a dead character without a good reason.

However, I think the scene with the Moon and Caine is quite sweet, especially after I heard someone say "As Gooseworx voices the Moon, this scene might also represent her love towards Caine".

When he finally saw the circus members again, he felt a bit worried if they would forgive him. He fixed the glitches on the circus members' body, and appeared in front of them. The people were wary of Caine at first, but Caine only came to make amendments, and even brought a gift for them: A video detailing their true identity and their real counterpart's life after their copies were stuck in the circus.

One by one, each member's names got revealed. Pomni (Abigail) was still a accountant, but has taken up photography as a new hobby. Ragatha (Suzie) managed to leave her family, and continue her business. Gangle (Zoey), after recovering from the truck accident, decided to quit her manager job and persue her dreams of making her manga series. Zooble (Riley) had their own bar, and it came quite successful. Kinger (Grant) had a happy family with his wife, and has several children. Jax (Leeroy) left his home and moved to his friend's home, worked as a mailman and sometimes visits Riley's bar, a really funny coincedence. All of them were living a happy life outside. While the people in the circus were happy to see their real-life counterparts doing well, Pomni came up and said they aren't the same as the counterparts anymore, "In here, I'm just Pomni".

I wasn't actually expect to see real life scenes in the series, or even have their real life names and counterparts shown at all. However, I feel this might be the best way to resolve the whole story, Caine has been hiding so much secrets from them, the only way to resolve their conflict is to have Caine tell the whole truth. The character development of Pomni is really great as well. At the beginning, Pomni was the character who wants to leave the most, but slowly she learnt that leaving isn't a feasible option, and the fact she got friends who support her, she developed into a character who made peace being inside the circus, and learns the ways to live happily there.

Not everyone liked the ending where the characters gets stuck in the circus for eternity and have to cope with being fine with it, but for me, I think this kind of ending is quite unique. As much as being trapped away from your original home seems dreadful at first, the show wants to tell us that when there are things we can't achieve, instead of chasing after the unobtainable goal, we should look in a different perspective and find the good things we have right now. While being trapped in a digital circus doesn't actually happen in real life (at least from what I'm aware), there are a lot of things that can majorly change our direction in life, like illnesses, accidents, financial difficulties. While we can't revert the change itself, there are ways we can improve our situation.

It also highlights the importance of human connection. For the majority of the character's internal conflicts, they don't get resolved on their own, but they have to connect with another human being. My favorite scenes in TADC are the ones characters sit down and talk about their feelings, like Pomni talking to Gummigoo about being real, Kinger giving encouragement to Pomni, and Kinger giving advice to Ragatha, and these all lead to changes in the characters. And for Jax, he tried to avoid any human connection, and as a result, he becomes more and more toxic and depressed, showing that humans can't live without being vulnerable.

In the end, we get to see the characters having fun, and alone with Caine this time! They renovated the circus, offered a place where abstractions can peacefully stay, and got to geniunely have fun. In the final scene, Pomni sits at the dinner table, which is a callback to EP1, but instead of having a panic attack, she calmly picks up her utensils and starts eating, concluding the story and Pomni's character development. Oh also Abstragedy sex is real.

Like I mentioned several times during my review, the movie isn't perfect, some of the decisions are strange, the series should've been a bit longer, and I feel like the recaps are too long. But if I had to give a score, it would be 8/10. The flaws are there, but they achieve the overall theme, they landed the ending quite peacefully. There isn't much grand lore reveals aside from the brain scans part, but I think this is the point. Ever since EP2, I learnt that the point of TADC isn't about lore uncovering, but the character developments and connections.

In a time where ongoing series tend to get crappy and/or rushed endings, I think TADC's ending is pretty good, you can see how much I enjoy it by looking at my word length of this blog. Now that TADC has ended, I might look for new series to follow, not sure if I would choose a series from Glitch Studios considering they've broken a contract with a Korean theater, and I don't want to see any more discourse.
