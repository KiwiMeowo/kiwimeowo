My sister recommended me the anime Gnosia, it's a werewolf game style visual novel adapted into an anime. While I enjoy stories with the character's life at stake, the story isn't about winning the game; It's about learning about the characters and lore.

Spoilers for the entire anime and game

Werewolf game

In case you don't know what is the Werewolf game, it's a game where players are divided into villagers and werewolves, villagers have to defeat the werewolves, and werewolves, who pretend to be villagers, need to kill the villagers. Every game cycle, werewolves will wake up whilst the villagers are sleeping, and they will kill one villager. When morning comes, villagers have to discuss who are the werewolves. After discussing, they will vote who to execute. When all werewolves are killed, the villager wins; If the amount of werewolves are equal to the number of villagers, the werewolves win. It's a game requiring high attention to body language and ability to lie, this is also why I'm bad at this game.

Little off topic from Gnosia, there's also a range of roles for the villagers:

    Villagers

  • Fortune teller: Check the role of a player every round
  • Witch: Has one healing potion and one bottle of poison, and is notified who gets killed each night. They can use the healing potion to revive them, and can also use the poison to kill any player.
  • Hunter: When they are eliminated, they can choose to kill one person
  • Sheriff: Their vote will worth 1.5x more than a regular vote, if the sheriff dies, they can either pass it onto another player, or forfeit the role entirely.

    • Werewolves

  • Wolf king: If they get killed, they can drag someone along with them.

    • Third party

  • I remember there's a role where their goal is to be killed, I thought it's called the Idiot, but apparently the Idiot's role is something else entirely. I still don't know what the role is called.
  • Cupid: Choose two players to be a couple, if both players are villagers, the three players will be on the villagers' team, and vice versa. If one is a villager while the other is a werewolf, they will be on their own team. (My least favorite role, it serves almost nothing other than shipping purposes, says a lot on how I dislike romance)

There are many other roles, but these are the ones I remember playing with.

Back to the story

So the protagonist (called Yuri in the anime) wakes up in a space ship, finding himself with several crew mates. One of them is infected with Gnosia, who the infectees will eliminate humans during space warp or when the number of gnosia overtakes the humans. The crew members have to vote out one person to send them to cold sleep, and if all gnosia infectees are found, the humans win.

In the first episode of the anime, Setsu gives Yuri a strange artifact before they get sent off to cold sleep, which was the thing that give him the ability (or perhaps curse?) for time loops. While it gives Yuri another chance to try again, the journey will be very very long.

Interesting twists on the genre

Getting a bit more spoilery, there are a lot of things that makes it stand out than just a werewolf-game-style anime:

The aim isn't to win the games, but to find out new knowledge

The artifact, called the Silver Key, which causes the time loops seeks out new knowledge, with every thing Yuri learns from the crew mates, it gets filled up more. It is when it fills up completely, that the time loops actually end.

The infectee is different every loop

After the first round, you might assume the infectee is the same next round, but it isn't the case. Because the time loops aren't necessarily time loops, Yuri was actually jumping from timelines to timelines, and each timelines differ slightly. This is also why the number of crew mates, who the crew mates are, the roles, and the infectees are different each time.

Setsu also experiences time loops

The person who gives Yuri the Silver Key actually has one themself, and is also experiences time loops. However, the time loops Yuri and them experience isn't synced up. For example, Yuri might be having his 42th time loop, and he might be talking to Setsu experiencing their 69th time loop. And when Yuri is experiencing his 670th time loop, the Setsu he sees probably only had their 4th time loop (I made up those numbers).

In the first few episodes, the anime focuses on playing the werewolf game, but eventually it focuses more on learning about the characters. And eventually, Yuri learns that specific character combinations and various factors can trigger specific situations, such as new relations, backstory explaination, romance routes etc.

Some character and episode (routes?) thoughts

I only watched the anime, and haven't checked out all the routes yet, but there are characters I do enjoy quite a lot, especially Raqio. It's a recurring thing where they accuses Yuri for being suspicious, but since being too accusing makes one more suspicious, they also get voted off in the first round very frequently (which gets funnier every time lmao). On the other hand, they are actually really smart, when Yuri makes a mistake, they will take advantage of that and point out all the flaws he made, causing him to lose. They are also the one with the most knowledge on how the Silver Key works, helping Yuri advance in his time loops.

The route I found the most memorable is when Kukrushka murders every one. I wasn't particularly surprised by the twist of the innocent looking girl being evil, but this was surely terrifying anyway. With most routes, when Yuri gets killed or wins the game, the game restarts and he starts over. In this route, everyone except Remnan and Kukrushka (who are 100% humans) goes to cold sleep. The ending song plays, expecting for the episode to end, but it gets corrupted within a few seconds.

Yuri gets waken up by Remnan, who warns of Kukrushka trying to kill everyone. Kukrushka comes into the room with cold sleep pods, and smashes every single one along with the crew mates still inside. Gina tried to escape the ship, but Kukrushka force-opens the airlock before Gina could put on her helmet. Yuri and Remnan made it to escape pod, Remnan explained that Kukrushka acts the same as his former captor, someone very horrible. The escape pod counts down, "Three... Two... One... One... One...", but gets stuck on the final second. The wall gets smashed down, revealing Kukrushka, who is covered in blood, a creepy ost plays.

Ok seriously this is just a horror movie scene at this point.

There's also the part about the story's non-binary gender: Pangender (Or Han/Hansei). The definition is close to being what we know as agender, pangender are often seen as aroace as well, but it isn't necessarily the case. Hansei people might also go through gender-affirming surgery, but Raqio stated one's gender has nothing to do with one's physical sex, han being no exception... Damn I'm surprised an anime would even touch on the topic of gender like this.

Though there are some stuff I do feel complicated about, such as how easily the characters would go to the cold sleep pods. It is stated that cold sleep pods are somewhat risky, so it is a generally bad idea to just let everyone inside the cold pod. But when someone gets voted off, most of them would just get inside without much struggle, with the worst case being Sha-Ming begging for mercy if he gets voted off (god he's pathetic). But I honestly imagined at least one person would just try to run away from their fate, while everyone else tries to capture them. It feels weird that even the infectees, who are stated to be hostile, are still willing to go inside, sometimes even outside of voting.

There's also the thing about the roles. There's Engineer, Doctor and Guardian Angel, which helps the humans investigate who's human or infectee. And yet, it just so happens that there's not enough powers for the roles to be used more than once in one round... That's awfully convenient!

Ending

In a route where there is 0 infectees, it feels like there's a happy ending. But what's unusual is that, there's another Yuri inside the medical pod, and the moment their eyes meet, the universe ends. Yuri (the one experiencing the time loop) realises that he is a Bug, an error made by the universe after his original was eliminated by Gnosia (wait then why didn't the Gnosia infectees point out "Yuri" surviving their attack?). He was stuck in the exact same timeline for many times, slowly going mad over the fact he isn't the real Yuri. After many attempts to break the loop and killing himself (there's timelines where the crew mates sees him killing himself for unknown reason, let that sink in), he learns from Raqio that the reason he kept looping back to the same state is because the Silver Key is seeking some info that only this timeline has: Which is Yuri himself. After he eavesdrop from the awakened Yuri, he was finally free from this timeline.

The next time loop, which is his last one, is the route with every character on the ship, preparing for take off. It was also the loop where he meets pre-timeloop Setsu, being completely distant towards Yuri. The ship is suddenly damaged, debris collpasing onto Setsu, Yuri gives a Silver Key (which is given by Raqio the GOAT) to Setsu, preventing them from dying and starting their timeloop. It's just like the chicken or egg question, did Setsu cause the timeloops for Yuri first, or did Yuri cause the timeloops for Setsu first? That's a question you will fall into an infinite loop, I love it.

When Yuri's Silver Key finally fills up, he and Setsu goes back to the 0 infectee route. The Silver Key opens up a gate to another universe, and Setsu brings the medical pod, containing a sleeping Yuri, to the portal. The reason Setsu can't stay is because they haven't completed their own loop yet, and also by taking away the duplicate Yuri, he can stay in this world without the universe ending itself. After they leave, Yuri meets the other crew mates again, except none of them remember Setsu.

And somehow, this isn't the end for Setsu. When Yuri discussion the situation with Gina, she becomes confused, "How can they finish their own timeloop if they need to hand over the Silver Key to you?". I still struggle a bit about why this is a problem, won't the universe create a new Bug Yuri? But now I think it might be because in the first loop Yuri experiences, is supposed to be the second last loop Setsu experiences. In order for Setsu to obtain their last piece of information, the timelooped Yuri has to return to their timeline. So Yuri decides to use a new Silver Key, suffer from the timeloops again, went to Gnos, entered a portal with the filled key, then went to meet Setsu.

When Yuri reunits with Setsu, they are extremely surprised, but that's the piece they need to brak their timeloop. However, Yuri decides to use a new Silver Key on himself, and that would finally wipe out his existence (I actually don't understand why). The meeting is very simple, since there's only 5 of them and the Gnosia is already found, which is SQ, or Manan in SQ's body. That meeting ends without any casualties though, Setsu proposed Manan to possess a spare Kukrushka doll, then she could go through the portal made by Setsu's key, allowing for the route where Kukrushka kills everyone... Man this time loop shit is insane.

In Yuri's final moments, Setsu and him lie down on the floor as the ships goes through a warp. They look at each other with joy, staying together in peace...

I'm not very smart, it took me multiple wiki visits to even understand what's going on, I still don't understand if all Kukrushkas are Manan, or if only the one who kills everyone is Manan. But seriously the anime is really interesting, even if there's frequent boob and ass shots on SQ. I haven't watched much footage on the game, but it is probably very fun as well, though I think I'm not smart enough for this. I haven't had this much fun watching a show, I always get horribly traumatised or get bored out of my mind, so I really recommend this anime.
