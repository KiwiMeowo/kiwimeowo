TADC: The Last Act, Part 1

Absolute cinema

Weather: Hot as hell

Listening to: TADC - Running the Show

Mood: Calm

Random thought: Sign I wish to rewatch the episode rn

Tags: Review, Fixation
987 words (4 min read)

Yesterday, I went to watch TADC: The Last Act with my sister, and it was a great time! This blog does not contain EP9 spoilers, only some small thoughts and feelings about the movie.

Just like the TADC movie did, here's a recap on the last few months regarding the movie! Back in April, TADC teased EP9, also known as The Last Act, and it would come out as a movie. Unfortunately, it was only available at Japan, America and Canada at the time, and those who couldn't go to the cinemas will have to wait for the Youtube/Netflix release, which comes out two weeks after the cinema release.

As upset as fans were, many of us tried our best to contact cinemas to screen this movie, Hong Kong fans included! Lucky for us, the arrangements were made, and global synchronised screening was confirmed. Being a person who loves observing fandom activities, I booked the earliest screening possible, which starts at 11pm at my time, in a cinema an hour away from my home. Worth it though.

Waiting time

On the day of screening, I initally went outside with my regular clothes, but eventually decided to change into better clothes because I don't want to look like someone who hasn't touched grass in years (And wore a hat as well cuz my hair was getting oily). I also got there 1.5 hours early, just to observe the fans. I got there a bit too early, since there weren't much people gathering at that moment. But as the time approaches 11pm, more and more people are outside the cinema, many of them noticeably carrying TADC merch (Which made me regret not buying any merch).

When the contacting cinemas thing started, I joined a Hong Kong TADC Discord server so that Hong Kong fans can work in unison. I won't say I'm very active in here, but it makes me happy to see people who live in the same place as me chatting the same interests. While I was standing outside the cinema, I was watching the chat, seeing if I can recognise them in the public. In the end, I never spoke to any of them, being the social-awkward that I am. I hope they had fun though...

After a lot of waiting, the staff there started ticket checking, and immediately a long queue formed. Despite me and my sister standing near the cinema entrance, because of how fast people were moving, we ended up in the far back LMAO. When it's our turn to enter, the staff gave us one sticker set each. And a small good gesture that the staff did was they distriuted the two sticker sets alternately, like if you received set 1, the next person will receive set 2, then the next set 1 again... Which means me and my sister got both sets!

As the fans slowly entered their seats, I watched the people around me. I really loved how some people dressed up for the occasion, not only are there fans who wore TADC shirts, there are also people who dressed up similarly to the TADC characters, or even cosplayers!

The Amazing Digital Recap

As you already know, the first part of the movie is an recap of EP8, so I won't spoiler anything about it. When the movie started, people clapped at the Glitch intro, which I feel quite amazed, like wow people are EXCITED for the movie! There's some bonus stuff at the beginning (which I won't say), but when it gets into the EP8 recap, I tried my best to remain excited while watching the parts that I've rewatched multiple times. Even until now, I still feel like making almost half of the movie being content fans have definitely watched is a bit... unwise. Regardless, I patiently waited for new content while feasting on the popcorn me and my sister were sharing.

During the part when Caine started singing, I was half-expecting the audience to start singing or clapping... but it didn't happen. In fact, the fans there were very well-behaved, only short laughs and giggles during jokes, aside from that they remained as quiet as possible! While I feel happy about the fans here having good etiquette when so many people online vented about their screening being interrupted by screaming kids, I kinda wished the fans sang together in this section, especially how all of us (maybe) knew what happens next in the next 30 minutes-ish.

After around 45 minutes the movie started, Caine gets deleted and Zooble realises they can swear. At this point, I completely forgot about the popcorn, and locked in watching the new plot...

Some feelings

Of course, I can't say anything about EP9 yet, I made a digital pinky promise, you see! However, I do have some spoiler free impressions, but if you want to watch the movie without being tainted by anyone's opinions, I've spoilered it, just in case: From what I've seen on Twitter, many people who watched the leaks are very dissatisfied about the ending. I never watched the leaks, so I can't say what went down or if the leaks were true. But for my impressions after watching the movie? Really good! The movie isn't perfect, there are flaws I want to point out, but overall it ties to the overall theme well! In the day and age where stories tend to end in a half-assed and/or unsatisfying note, I feel like TADC's ending is well done, every core message that needs to be said has been said.

For the spoilery stuff, I'll save the until the day the Youtube release is out. Until then, I'll just prepare for my trip, maybe start writing my more detailed review...
