Late ass Pride Month post

Imposter syndrome and such

June is Pride Month, the time people celebrate their identities. As someone who is demisexual, it would makes sense if I also do something about it this month. Yet I hesitate, in fact I still have trouble registering that I'm part of the LGBTQ+.

Like I said in my past posts, I have figured out that I'm demisexual, or at least it's 99% likely. However, it's difficult for me to truly accept myself. I mentioned in my last Pride Month blog that one of the reasons I don't feel the need to come is that doing so doesn't impact much in my life. But there's another deeper reason to it, I'm afraid of being ridiculued. It's the same for many queer people, they might live in a hostile environment and don't feel safe to express themselves. But for me, I feel like if I came out, I would risk being a target for nothing. Even if I don't come out, I feel quite comfortable with the current lifestyle, I don't need to pretend to like people I'm not interested in, I don't need to force myself to date or marry people I don't like, the people who know me (likely) all assume I'm a non-LGBT person. But if I come out, nothing would change aside from the fact people would make fun of me for trying to be special about "being normal".

Another thing is that many people seems to misunderstand being ace. It seems that the common concept of being ace is that ace people are sex-repulsed and only enjoys other hobbies and such. While it might be the case for some ace people, it doesn't apply to everyone, they might feel sexual attraction in specific scenarios, it might come and go randomly, or maybe they don't feel attracted to people but still enjoy sexual acts. This concept apparently perplexes a lot of people, sometimes people in the LGBTQ+ also. Everytime I see people say something along the lines of "You aren't ace if you experience sexual attraction" or "You are just mentally ill", it makes me fear expressing myself more. Sometimes I think to myself that if I have other sexual attractions, it would be easier to explain myself.

Moreover, I sometimes feel like I'm not demisexual. There are times that I experience something that doesn't really fit the definition of being demisexual, while it is possible that ace people can experience attraction, or maybe I'm still on the ace spectrum but actually is on another label, but I might feel wrong to claim myself to be ace, or even in the LGBTQ+ community. This is perhaps the reason I made this post so late, I feel like an imposter among the community, invading minorities' space when their situation is already tough.

But uh, this is Pride Month. I feel I should be like, more proud of how I love. Even after what I said here, it wouldn't feel right to tell someone else that they aren't xxx. I should learn and tell myself that there's nothing wrong about figuring stuff out, and there's nothing wrong with being ace and stuff.

Uh yea, happy pride.
