The days when I would dig for one day early info

Weather: Kinda hot

Listening to: The Amazing Digital Circus

Mood: Exhausted

Random thought: Ughhh there's so much laundry

Tags: Life
667 words (3 min read)

Being in various fandoms, I was an excitable fan who would go through every corners of the Internet just to get a small info on the newest updates. Even if said info is something meant to be officially released the very next day, it still brought me so much thrill.

Back when I was interested in the Danganronpa series, I was in the time before DRV3 hasn't been released. Just a day before it got released, several leaked images appeared in Tumblr. I was actually trying to avoid spoilers to get the shock of who dies, but the feeling of seeing something without knowing the full context is kinda thrilling. Yea, the character died, but how did that happen? They died so horrificly, how would the story convey it? The fact we don't know the full story yet just makes us more nervous. These are the questions that appeared in my head when I saw the leaks.

This happened similarly in other fandoms I was in, comic panels without context, storylines that may or may not be fake, new characters... Sometimes these leaks require people to dig deep into websites that you might not been to before, there's a Chinese website that I forgot the name, but I used to search for new comic updates there. I somehow had to patience to dig page to page, but not wait another day for the full release.

Anyways, the reason I remembered this experience is because allegedly, Tomodachi Life: Living the Dream got leaked, and some group extracted ROM for the game. Several Tomodachi Life accounts mentioned this news, many fans rushed to ask where the leaks are.

As someone who is a fan of this game, I went to search for leaks, not for myself to play, but to watch people play. In my past experiences, if there are leaks, it wouldn't be too difficult to find tiny snippets of leaks by simply looking up in social media. But the strange thing is that, there are only people asking where the leaks are, and no signs of screenshots and new info. After digging for a few minutes, I had the hunch that the leaks aren't even there.

In some of the tweets I've looked at, it is rumored that the leaks are found in 4chan. While 4chan has a bad reputation, I used to visit there for Houseki no Kuni chapter leaks (I still remember the false rumor of Rutile suddenly falling into a coma LMAO). As I visited several of the threads, all I saw was 4chan users being confused as well, while saying slurs of course.

There's also a guy who kept sending a Discord link, but I never joined the server as it felt suspicious. Joining the server itself doesn't bring much harm, it's only until you send your private infor that it becomes problematic, but I'd rather not join it to not give the potential scammer attention. It's also it mentioned "Install with caution. Do not share link publicly" as if they aren't sharing the server link to public. But according to some tweets and 4chan, the server owner claimed to have the xci/nsp, but never shared it, and instead made the members raid some other server.

Aside from that server, I've seen some people claiming to have the leaks, but kept stalling time. "I need more time to extract the data. Oh no I lost progress, give me another hour lmao lmao", it is clear they just want to get some internet attention. There are people sending a download link for Tomodachi Life, except it was still the demo version. Some users did do the fake leaks thing funnier though, like that one who sent a Rickroll link.

It is clear I have been scrolling too much Twitter lately, I had the rule for myself that I can only scroll Twitter if there are major news for my interests... But they are appearing nonstop this month, this can't be good for my health.
