Because we cannot have nice things, everyone has to receive meaningless cold calls or scam calls every day. No matter if you are a worker who has to pay attention to every phone call, or a person who fears the sound of the phone ringing, you definitely HATE these spam calls, they waste your energy and time. So yea this is a post venting about it.

I, too, suffer from the endless spam phone calls. Despite the government's efforts, the policies they made has proven uneffective in stopping or reducing them, and I receive multiple meaningless calls every day. When I first started university, I downloaded the spam-blocking app to block any suspicious phone numbers. But after my job-hunting journey began, I have to remove the block function to avoid accidentally blocking the companies who may call me back.

The phone calls had gotten so bad that I managed to remember some of the calls by the first few words; Yes, many of the spam calls use pre-recorded audio instead of real humans. My pattern-seeking skills did help me waste less time and immediately end the call, and even if the call I received doesn't match any of the calls I've memorised, it is easy to tell if the audio is pre-recorded by waiting a few seconds without talking, they would just go on and on until the listener from the other side realise something's up and end the call themself.

However, there are times that a real human talks to me, one possibility is that they are a cold caller that promotes some product I don't need, perhaps some kind of service from a bank, which every time I'd reject or just end the call. If they aren't salesmen, their speech pattern would feel different, they actually wait for your words. A major difference between a spam call and a hiring company is that company would start with asking me if I'm [real name].

While this information may be false, I always have this worry that the spam calls would record my voice and train an AI to replicate my voice to do more scams. I don't think simply saying "Hello", "Yes" and "No" would give much training data, but just in case I often try my best to avoid talking to them. If they do record my voice, I want to do something funny, like playing some random video to waste their time, or do what others did: put the phone under a wok and whack it as loudly as possible.

I hate phonecalls after all those years of being spam called by scams and my emotionally unable parents, I wish I get some peace being alone.
