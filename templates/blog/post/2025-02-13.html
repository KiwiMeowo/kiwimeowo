Growing up, I noticed plenty of adults likes drinking alcohol for some reason. Despite how people described it to be bitter and harsh on the mouth, they often continue to drink it, and I never really know why they like it so much.

I had alcohol multiples times, less than 10 cups I think. One of those times are during my childhood, when I mistook rice wine for water during a trip of Korea. It was extremely bitter, I was quite disgusted. The next time I had another cup of alcohol is during Orientation Night, where university students hang out and stuff. In my point of view, beer taste like rotten orange juice, it was so bitter I couldn't take more than a small sip each time. The people there said my alcohol tolerance isn't quite good, but I think I just ingested it very slowly and I actually only took a little amount. I also had fruit soju, which taste just like fruit juice, and taste quite nice with yakult. But honestly, the taste feels just like juice I'd rather just drink normal juice at that point.

If alcohol taste so bad, then why do people like it so much? That is a question I often have through out my life. Ok back to alcohol. Another response people gave was that alcohol numbs their minds. Because alcohol makes people drunk, they often forgets their worries and pain, and just let their minds sink into whatever happens next. Knowing that, it makes me wonder how being drunk feels. With the few times I've drunk alcohol before, I always consume a reasonable amount because it isn't exactly safe to be drunk there. But I lowkey wish I know how being drunk feels, just to maybe experience something cool. I kind of worry what drunk me would do though, I heard some people become very violent under alcohol.
