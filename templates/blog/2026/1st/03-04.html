Ah yes, the dreaded Homestuck. It isn’t very popular in Hong Kong, but it’s everywhere in western countries from the stories I’ve heard. My initial impressions of this series is just I found it funny when people cower in fear when the word Homestuck is mentioned. But sometime in the COVID19-era, I decided to give this webcomic a try.

One thing to note is I haven’t read Homestuck for a long time, so I might be incorrect about some info. Like always, feel free to correct any mistakes.

If you don’t know, Homestuck is a multimedia webcomic, featuring four humans playing a game called Sburb, which they didn’t know it would destroy their homes once it’s launched. Following this story, there’s also aliens, time shenanigans, and generally wacky events and plot twists.

What I enjoy about this webcomic is the enormous world building. Within the first few chapters, you will learn the mechanics of Sburb and the characters, like the inventory system, the Kernal Sprites, planets etc.

While the troll romace system is described to be very weird, I lowkey find it very unique and interesting. As you know, I’m a romance hater, and hates corny romace scenes. But with this romance quadrant system, it introduces romance^2, featuring hate love, BFF love and hate love manager (?), it really gives a breathe of fresh air to what we knew of romace.

Speaking of trolls… I actually have little to no interest in them, and I prefer watching the beta humans do stuff. Not because the trolls are boring, they do have a lot of lore, but their typing quirks are so difficult to read that my brain simply gives up. While trolls such as Karkat and Kanaya is easier to understand (It’s just consistent capitalisation), any typing quirks that replaces any characters with other symbols/numbers makes it hard to read tenfold. Typing quirks, or leet speech, can be fun if it’s just a few lines of it, but as someone who has trouble reading long stories, it’s painful.

And now to the part about its length, which I heard is even longer than the Bible. I’m not a fan of reading books, likely because of school. I also have trouble reading long-form stories, as mentioned above. When I try to read many lines of words, my brain skips more and more words as time progresses. It may be skill issue, and I should’ve taken a break instead of binging the entire thing in the span of 3-4 days, vast majority of this experience is somewhat tiring, and I just wanted to get over it.

I said enough about my negative feelings, now back to stuff I enjoyed. I find the first few chapters with the humans quite enjoyable. It’s simple in contrary to anything after the trolls, but I could still keep up with the dialogue (probably because my brain was still fresh). I liked watching the humans figure out the gameplay, and I laughed when John accidentally yeets an item out of the window because his inventory was full. I also really love the part when Rose enters Sburb, I was worried if she would die as I didn’t have any prior knowledge on Homestuck, so I was very excited watching the cutscene, the music and the whole thing was so good.

A character who I also liked is Becquerel, the omnipotent dog. He’s a dog who can teleport, eat radioactive steak, and dodge bullets, and he also loved his owner, Jade.

Because of Vriska messing things up and putting John to sleep, noone was there to help Jade prototype her sprite. Bec decided to prototype himself as the sprite and helped Jade enter the Medium. An important thing about prototyping is that anything that you prototype into the sprite, it is also copied to the underlings and the wearer of the prototype ring, which was Jack Noir, one of the antagonists. The bad guys now are teleporting dog gods now, good luck.

But also because the fact Bec is loyal to Jade, despite how powerful Jack Noir was, he couldn’t bring himself to kill her. And when Jade died, he even brought her to her quest bed, which revives her. This has to be one of my favourite parts of Homestuck.

And that’s not all to the story, when a player dies on the quest bed, they ascend to god tier and merges with their dream self. So not only Jade gained her classpect, she’s also a teleporting dog god. It’s so impressive one key story can lead to more more cool plot development.

The scene where Jade ascends takes part in the Cascade cutscene, which I believe is one of, if not the most, iconic Homestuck moments. It was when the humans and the trolls’ plans come together, and you would see many major events happening together like a chain. And when the animation window zooms in, it felt so cinematic, I feel chills down my spine. I watched a small clip of it on Youtube, but I didn’t know it would do that on the Homestuck collection app, shame it’s being taken down by the author for some reason.

While there are many flaws to the series, and the author is kinda questionable, I appreciate the charm of this seriea. Would I recommend it?… I don’t know, but it certainly changed something in me, especially in my OC writings.
