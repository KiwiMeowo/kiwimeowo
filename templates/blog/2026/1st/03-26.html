So last September, the Ninendo Direct teased Tomodachi Life: Living the Dream. As someone who enjoys the Sims, Miitopia and the older version of Tomodachi Life, and someone who loves inserting my OCs into stuff, I was so excited for it's release. Not only that, they have added lots of improvements, such as gay marriage, island decoration, non-binary gender option, face painting??? Damn they went max on the woke factor, I love it lmao.

Yesterday, while I was browsing Twitter for TADC stuff, I suddenly got hit with the news of Tomodachi Life demo, IT'S HERE ALREADY!!! Sadly, the demo is only released on EU, well I guess we could only sit back, and watch the other people have fun on Youtube...

SIKE, if it's only on EU, just make an account on EU! So I quickly went to make a new account, set the icon to Mii Lavender, then quickly downloaded the demo off the shop. I'd imagine this moment to be me throwing Lavender to the United Kingdom to get me the game, funny as hell.

Anyways, how's the game?

So when I called my island "Ball Island", honoring the ball shaped Timekeeper, cuz why not. And of course, the first Mii is Cosmos (Timekeeper is the third one). I was surprised by so many options to give my Mii, there's so much more hair settings, you can even select the bangs and back hair seperately. What I love the most has to be the face painting, allowing me to draw cat ears and other decorations. It isn't as detailed as the one in Miitopia, but I find it quite nice.

I was shocked not only there's a non-binary gender option, you can also choose their sexual orientation, and pronouns that doesn't have to follow their gender. You can also select if the Mii's are familially related to other Miis on the island, so that they won't accidentally marry each other 💀. These things are what Tomodachi fans had been asking for so long, with so many things going against what we want nowadays, I'm surprised Nintendo did listen to them.

  1. You checked the dreaming Mii

You might ask, "Isn't the Chinese translation in Mandarin, not Cantonese?" You are correct, it's somewhat unfortunate. However, with some work arounds, you still can get the game to say Cantonese words. When you give your Miis names and phrases, you can set the pronunciations if it doesn't speak it correctly.

If you want to make them say "仆街", you can make the pronunciation as "Pouk gaei". For "屌你老母", the character 屌 isn't available on the Switch (They must have filtered it ), so you will have to type it as 吊, for pronunciation it will be like "Dee u lei low mow".
