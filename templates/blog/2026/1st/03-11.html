So let me explain briefly on what happened in the story. Dark Enchantress, after gaining her semi-human form, starts to attack the cookies. Having her enormous body and strength, nobody could stand a chance, many cookies (even Madeleine, Wildberry, Tea Knight etc) crumbled under DE's hands. Gingerbrave gets grabbed by DE, and she shoves him into the primodial oven, where the Ultimate Dough resides. Gingerbrave, watching his fallen friends from above, falls into despair.

Inside the oven, Gingerbrave meets the Ultimate Dough, who asks him to be merged with them. The Ultimate Dough convinces him that he can stop being just a common and boring cookie, and become stronger together, so that he can protect his friends and revenge on the witches. Gingerbrave is tempted at first, but decides that he wants to remain as himself, for he wouldn't be Gingerbrave anymore if he got combined.

Ultimate Dough becomes angry, the many souls within the dough starts speaking individually, like their minds are splitting apart. Then they chased after Gingerbrave, attempting to take over him by force. Gingerbrave tries to run, but he felt himself getting slower, as the Ultimate Dough wraps themselves around him...

Like a glimpse of afterlife people might see before death, Gingerbrave finds himself in a dark place shined by stars called Bravery Hill. There, he meets a familiar cookie, the cookie who he met in the form of a statue back in Chapter 2 called Little Giant Cookie. Little Giant explains Bravery Hill is where cookies of the same ingredients go to when they pass on or encounter near-death experiences, and these two cookies share the same recipe. Little Giant reminds Gingerbrave that even if he looks normal, he still has his incredible bravery which can change a lot. Gingerbrave hears his friends calling for him from outside, encouraged by Little Giant's words, he ran towards the door.

Ok, just let me explain why this story is so hated:

1. Sugar Swan being nerfed

Sugar Swan, as stated, is the life-bringer of Earthbread, a god as you would say. As powerful as DE is, it's too strange that SuSwan got defeated so easily, and she shouldn't be forgotten in the story so quickly, SHE'S A LEGENDARY!!!

2. Freedom means death

White Lily, whose half is a war criminal, feels responsible for her actions, and often believes the only way to repent is to die. Then she had her arc, where she confronts SiSalt and she decides to live on to bring cookies freedom. And now, she dies to achieve her freedom, themes and such. Many critiques by the fans is that it conveys the message that dying is a good way of being free, and somewhat implies suicide is a valid way of freeing from pain.

This reminds me of an analysis on Medusa, who got assaulted and turned into a monster as an punishment for being assaulted. The analysis said her story represents the hardships of being a woman, and it also mentioned her being killed is perhaps the best outcome for her, since she's finally free from the horrors of womanhood, and I was like "No, it isn't. It isn't remotely good either, don't tell me to kill myself (Sfx: Disgust on my own gender increased by 50%!)"

I honestly doubt this is the message Devsis wants to convey, but that's what happens to clumsily written plot. I wouldn't call it catastrophically bad, but it is mid-bad, cheap-bad, stuff you would see in an rushed series.

3. What's next for the cookies?

The arc just barely ended, so I wouldn't expect everything to be resolved immediately. HOWEVER, I find it EXTREMELY CRIMINAL that POMEGRANATE, the one who LOVES DE THE MOST, DOESN'T GET TO SAY ANYTHING AT THE ENDING. Like, White Lily says her dying words, dies, Shadow Milk appears, leaves after the Solidarity Cookie shows up, Shadow Milk cameo again, and it ends. Aside from Pure Vanilla, nobody else got to comment on the situation!

And as a Pome fan, I'm so mad she didn't get to have any dialog at the end. The whole story about Pome is that she wanted to get away from her village and her priesstress life, and DE gave her a vision where she can happily live in the world of darkness. Throughout the story, she's extremely loyal to DE, she would fight until she pass out, she would even sacrifice herself to make DE's plans come true.

Now that DE died, what would Pome do? From the credits art, we know Pome's devastated, the light in her eyes all gone. What would she do? Would she run away and hide in depression? Would she go mad and try to bring about the second coming of Dark Enchantress? There's a lot of interesting questions for Pome, and even the Cookies of Darkness.

But noOOoOo, Devsis is too busy milking their precious cash cow:

4. 1000 Bajillion stories for Shadow Milk!!!!!

It's already a long-existing issue since Shadow Milk debuted in the story, after Devsis realised his popularity, they kept shoving him into stories, no matter how connected he is to the storyline. I actually enjoyed this character at first, a charmful jester who always use theater analogies, but it gets old very quickly, especially since he occupies like 80% of the dialog.

And the fact he survives the story while the rest of the Beasts doesn't just shows how much Devsis favors him, and that he would continue annoy us in the next TBD arc. Want Timekeeper and the TBD to do more lore stuff? TOO BAD! Shadow Milk is here and will destory the world with his new soul jam powers! The Beasts are supposed to be a set, now it's just Shadow Milk Shadow Milk Shadow Milk Shadow Milk Shadow Milk Shadow Milk Shadow Milk-

The concept of a villain who is shown to be somewhat symphathetic and ends up doubling down IS interesting, but he has already have enough screentime, and there are too many characters who really needs more development. For example, Burning Spice is a Beast, a main character, and his concept is that he felt so stuck in the cycle of life that he felt the only way to escape from it is by destroying everything. If he has more care being put into, we would get to see more of his fears and insecurities, and even his realisation of destruction will eventually become another cycle. But now, he's just a brute who craves fights and violence.

There are plenty more complaints, but those are minor ones (like the Beasts' deaths being off-screen despite being important characters, IMO I think they won't have perma-death), or not exactly true (Like the ChatGPT accusations, but these aren't confirmed, and I lowkey think ChatGPT would do a better job).
