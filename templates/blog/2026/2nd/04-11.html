So this morning I woke up, I saw the trailer for EP9, A.K.A. the final episode of The Amazing Digital Circus, was out. I watched the trailer and was quite excited (Jax, I like your writing but I can't wait for you to die), but unfortunately... There's some bad news if you are not American, Latin American, Japanese or Canadian.

On June 4th, EP9 will be screened on cinemas in the countries mentioned, but it will only be released to Youtube and Netflix two weeks later. Of course, many fans are upset, they would have to spend two weeks avoiding spoilers on the Internet. And as someone who would avoid spoilers for new episodes of my fixations, avoiding spoilers is really hard. All social media is basically not safe anymore, especially if said series is popular. Even if you filter out all words related to the media, people would just post screenshots with only the caption "OMG NO WAY???" and it would still land on your timeline. Some people would just suggest "touching grass" for two weeks, but for some people, they have to use social media (eg work). And while, I did had the experience of not using social media to avoid spoilers, I don't think fans who are not impacted by this are allowed to say this, why not YOU don't post spoilers or "touch grass" for two weeks straight instead?

I wouldn't say I'm MAD mad, Hong Kong tends to be late to popular stuff overseas, or Hong Kong fans tend to be less vocal about their interests, so conventions and other fan events don't often occur here as much. Second, there is still a chance for theaters from other places to screen the movie, Glitch Studios recommended fans to tell their local theaters about this movie (Just like Iron Lung). And third, if worse comes to worst, I'll just watch a poorly recorded movie from someone sneaking a camera into the theater. However, I do feel bad for the fans who live in places where TADC isn't as popular, hence telling their local theaters won't be feasible. Moreover, I checked the release date for Iron Lung, it is first premiered January 30, 2026, and the earliest screening date in Hong Kong theaters is in March. FOMO will be hitting hard.

Regardless, I'm still attempting the method of contacting local theaters. In my POV, Hong Kong people tend to be more pessimistic, every time I see Hong Kong posts on Instagram, it's always about the government being a dumbass, or depressing news showing us show nothing matters. However, I had a strange urge to try anyways, worst case scenario is that theaters ignore me, best case scenario is that they start screening the movie, and I'm partially responsible for that change.

I had trouble finding Hong Kong TADC fans at first, I looked at Facebook, and the news wasn't spreading much at that time. I headed to Threads instead (I usually avoid there because it's more negative), and I finally found Hong Kong TADC fans telling everyone to send emails and start petitions. Seeing so many people uniting is such a encouraging sight, I know working together just for a show is kinda dumb, but it's the small things that teach us we have the ability to change for the better...

Anyways, I saw a couple people writing drafts for the emails, which encouraged me to do so as well. While typing the email, I felt mixed between embarrassment and awe, like damn I'm writing an email to say "There is a show I really like, can you screen the movie pretty pwease 🥺🥺🥺🥺🥺", hyperbole of course, I wrote in a semi-formal fashion. But that was way more than I expected I would do, it's kinda amazing I did that.

If the theaters responded to my email, or even agreed to show the movie, it will give me such much morale boost and excitement. But if not, I'll looks for random footages in the Internet.
