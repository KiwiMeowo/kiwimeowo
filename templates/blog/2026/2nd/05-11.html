As you may be aware few days ago, I had a crash out over CRK's story. After all my disappointment, I decided the way to regain my hope for the future is to enjoy a story that is actually good. It had been a while the last time I went to watch a movie, so I decided to pick a movie that is well-rated. While scrolling Twitter, particularly around the time when Atermis II launched, I occasionally saw people talking about Project Hail Mary. I hadn't watched much sci-fi movies, but the topic of space always intrigued me. So yea, I went to watch it with my boyfriend.

Before you read my poorly written review, I'll just say the movie is really good. You gotta watch it.

What I initially knew about the story is the protagonist is originally a teacher, but somehow ended up in a space mission to figure out how or why the sun and the stars are dimming out, there's also other aliens that are in the same mission. What interested me at first is the fact the humans aren't the only ones trying to save the stars, it makes sense that the advanced aliens who used to hide away in some corner of the space would come out. The human and aliens would also need to learn how to communicate with each other, trying to not let culture shock and communication barriers to cause misunderstanding and potentially start fights. I also thought this movie is about a person who somehow ended up being a astronaut despite the lack of qualifications. I watched a short clip of the movie, and I thought it would be a journey about how an ordinary person learns to be a hero or something.

Overall story

The story tells the journey of the protagonist, Ryland Grace, and how as he furthers his mission, his amnesia slowly subsides, remembering how he ended up in space. In the beginning of the movie, the tone is a bit melancholic, he woke up in a space ship alone, and two astronauts who were on the ship were dead. During the time he's trying to recover his memories, he also had to mourn for the two people who he barely knew. Moreover, he was years away from his planet Earth, how could he go back, or did he even have enough resources to go back? It shows how unforgiving the vastness of space was, making us feel the despair Grace was feeling.

The movie also showed snippets of Grace's memories. On Earth, he was a middle school teacher, who also used to be a controversial molecular biologist. He was suddenly hired to investigate astrophages, which were responsible for dimming the sun and other stars, and he became a part of Project Hail Mary. During the experiments and meetings, he figured out a way for the astrophages to reproduce, and learnt that they are also very potent fuels, suitable for the very immenent space mission. One important thing was that Grace was not part of the suicide mission, he should've been on Earth, so why did he end up there?

Despite the sad beginning, the tone changes when he suddenly met a giant spaceship outside of his own, he encountered a rock-like alien from Erid. Due to the language barriers and the strangeness of the situation, it took a while for the two to communiate. But the moment their trust was established was when the alien (who Grace nicknamed Rocky) built a human-friendly tunnel for him, Grace had little knowledge on whether if he misunderstood Rocky's message, or if Rocky was trying to harm him. But in the end, he decided to take off his helmet, showing how he trusts the new friend from outer space. In my mind, I was thinking how this act would be very risky in space missions, but I think this is what makes their friendship more special.

The next part of the story was how he started to live with Rocky in his spaceship while working on the mission together. While Grace made a program to translate Rocky's words and built a room for Rocky (Rocky cannot survive in Grace's atmosphere), they still struggled to be roommates. Nevertheless, they started to bond, they started to share their background to each other. Rocky eventually learns that Grace's space mission is a one way ticket, it has enough fuel to the Tau Ceti System, but not the way back. Rocky, who also lost his crewmates, did not want Grace to die alone, and decided to give him more fuel, in the cost Rocky would spend a few years more in space. The first half of the movie we learnt Grace might need to die in the end, but with this established, there's a new hope where he didn't need to abandon his hope of living on. The friendship between the two was very beautiful, I swear I heard other audiences were sobbing.

The two eventually learnt that one of the planets might have organisms that eat the astrophages, which means they could use it to hunt down the astrophages that are eating away the stars. They decided made a risky plan, they would put down a container which captures the microorganisms from the ship, which means they would need to travel at upper atmosphere, which might destroy the ship in the process. During the organism harvesting process, because of the spaceship crumbling from the pressure, it took a lot of trouble to even get the container back on the ship. When they finally managed to leave the planet's atmosphere, two of the fuel tanks started to leak, causing the ship to spin uncontrollably and Grace became unconscious. In order to save him, Rocky broke out of his protective case and dragged Grace to safety.

Grace woke up, but Rocky fell into a coma. During the time Rocky was sleeping, Grace was breeding the Taumoeba to make them adapt to Venus' atmosphere. During this point, there is a small worry in my that Rocky won't wake up. It wasn't very clear whether the movie would end in a positive note or not, and it would be devastating if Rocky died, not only Grace would lose a friend, Rocky's species would not survive either. But eventually, Rocky woke up, thank god.

When Rocky was giving a tour in his ship to Grace, a beam of rainbow light shone onto his face, giving him his final piece of missing memory. In the final days before the mission, the laboratory exploded due to the miscalculation of the astrophage amount, killing the crew's scientists and the other backup. Unfortunately, the only suitable canditate left was Grace, which he strongly refused to go. In the end, it wasn't his courage or sense of heroism that made him go to space, he was forced to go. The people who he befriended in the project watched him be drugged and put into a coma, and Grace, who was pinned to the ground, looked into the sky one last time, a rainbow was shining above.

In the scenario where the all humans are at risk are dying, it seems logical to sacrifice only one person instead of like 8 billion. But Grace isn't a brave hero, he was just an ordinary person who doesn't have the mindset of the other crewmates. It might seem cowardly and selfish for him to not do anything about the Earth, but isn't it also selfish for humanity to force him to die against his will? Humanity did not have other choice, but how justified is humanity for sacrificing an innocent person?

Thanks to Rocky's fuel, Grace now had a chance to return to Earth. The two parted aways, and headed home... Except suddenly, Grace got waken up by an alert about comtaminants. It turned out that Taumoeba managed to pass through the Xenonite container that Rocky uses and was beginning to eat away the fuel. While Grace managed to fix the issue, it wouldn't be the case for Rocky, whose ship is entire made of Xenonite. Grace could go back to Earth, but Rocky would be stranded in space and die a slow, painful death... Grace finally made a decision, he sent the Taumoabas and other materials back on Earth, then turned his ship to the direction where Rocky was heading...

On Earth, the items were safely delievered, helping the project continue. And back on Grace's point of view, he woke up next to a shore. It might seem like he's back on Earth, but he's actually on Erid, living in a biodome the Eridians built him, he got to teach the young Eridians just like what he did on Earth. Rocky told him that the Eridian scientists were working to send him back to Earth, but it seemed Grace might not want to.

What I thought really sad is that the people in the project thought he was dead, they would spend the rest of their lives thinking they sent him to die, and that guilt will haunt them to the very end. But perhaps it might be a good thing for Grace to stay on Erid, he had no direct family, nor much friends on Earth, and those he bonded with in the mission betrayed him for the greater good. The one he trusted the most now was Rocky, home is where the heart is, and perhaps he's found his home in Erid.

Final thoughts

The movie started depressing, but ended in a hopeful tone. Kinda regret I didn't watch it earlier, it's a great movie. I didn't read the book, but from what I learnt from the movie, it felt like there's some missing info or some more weird thoughts I had.

It is never explained how the other crewmates died, why did only Grace survive? I actually had a small memory of someone discussing the topic, so it might be paraphrasing. When Grace was discussing their missions with Rocky, it turns out the Eridians had no knowledge in topics such as relativity and radiation, and because of the ship's design, the crewmates likely died of radiation. And it might be possible something similar happened to Grace's ship as well, there might be some topic in science humans had not figured out yet, which might be the cause of the two crewmates' deaths. Honestly it is kinda haunting the fact it's remained unknown.

After the astrophage situation on the sun is dealt with, what would the humans do with the rest of them? I really doubt they would simply discard them since they are really good fuel, they could continue to be fuel for future space missions, or electricity, or if the governments are thinking awful ideas: war. If every country knew about astrophages and learnt to harvest them, shit will surely go down, one humanity-ending problem solved, then another arises. But I doubt this kind of ending is what the story strives for, it ending at Grace having a peaceful life on Erid is enough.

Perhaps I should go watch more movies during this free time.
