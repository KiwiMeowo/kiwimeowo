As you may already know, I am a mostly traditional artist. The reason I prefer drawing on paper instead of on mobile devices is because it just... feels better. My fingers are too stubby to draw proper lines on my phone, there is always a dissonance between the screen, my Wacom tablet and my hands, and there's so much settings on apps, it gets so overwhelming I'd rather stick to something more simple.

However, lately, I have to change my habits a bit. I store each of my drawings using folders and binders, and as they are physical items, they occupy space. Thinking about the long term, if I keep drawing on paper, my room would one day run out of space. As much as I enjoy traditional drawing, I feel like I have to switch to an alternative, which I might possibly have already...

When I first started university, it gave me a free Ipad for educational purposes. Before the time I needed laptops for projects, I always brought my Ipad for music, Youtube and other casual stuff... Yes I use it more for personal stuff than educational ones. But because I started using my laptop more, my Ipad is basically unused for a long time. Recently, I found out a use for this Ipad, which is doodles and drawings, and the only missing key is the Apple Pencil.

Unfortunately, as Apple products tend to do, they are expensive, the first gen Apple Pencil is HKD$779, and I don't want to pay that much for a pencil. So I decided to buy a fake one off Taobao, it might not work as well, but they should be functional, right? So after a few days, the stuff are delivered, I tested my pencil, and it worked as intended! Except one thing, which is pen pressure. It's not like pen pressure is necessary for me to draw, but it feels off to see the lines don't get lighter and smaller when I draw lightly, it's harder to draw details. So my next goal is to find a way to activate the pen pressure settings.

At first, I had no idea why the pen pressure is not on. I've tried turning my Ipad off and on and reactivating Bluetooth, I also bought a slightly more expensive fake pencil, but none of these methods worked. After a bit more researching, I figured out that pen compatibility might be the issue here. Different versions of Apple pencils might work for certain versions of Ipads, and since I use gen 9 Ipad, I would have to use gen 1 Apple Pencil, and I have no idea what the fake Apple Pencils' versions are. And what's annoying is that all the cheap pens have ambiguous versions, and I have to pay HKD$100-ish to get the gen 1 version.

And suddenly, I remembered something: My sister has a gen 1 Apple Pencil! And since she doesn't draw, she likely doesn't need the pen pressure settings at all. I asked her if she can swap her Pencil with my fake one, and she said to just take hers since she doesn't use it much. I tested the Pencil, and the pen pressure worked perfectly! Take that, Apple!

There's also another smaller issue regarding drawing on my Ipad, which is the apps. I usually use Medibang to draw, but ever since a certain time, the number of ads significantly increased, and many brushes (including the oil pastel) became unavailable unless you watch ads (3 hours of use time only) or upgrade to premium version. The fact Medibang Premium is subscription-based means I would not touch it in a ten mile radius, so I would find a way to get around it. While some of my most used brushes are blocked, i found other brushes that work well for me as well.

And for the ads that pop up every three seconds, you can disable them if you just turn off Wi-Fi. It's a bit annoying to toggle Wi-Fi whenever I open Medibang, but I found out it can be an automatic process using Shortcuts. I wanted the Bluetooth to turn on only when I open Medibang as well since I don't want the Pencil to drain batteries when it's unused, but it seems I have to manually pair the Pencil again after turning off Bluetooth anyways.

I honestly was considering to buy Procreate, but I already have CSP on my laptop, and I mainly want to draw on my Ipad for doodles anyways. Well here's the end of my Apple Pencil saga, look forward to digital doodles.
